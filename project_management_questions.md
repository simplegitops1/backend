## Section D: Case Study Questions

### Case Study 1: E-Learning Platform Development
**Scenario:** A university wants to develop a comprehensive e-learning platform with the following features:
- Video lectures and live streaming
- Assignment submission and grading
- Discussion forums
- Quiz and examination system
- Student progress tracking
- Mobile app support

**Timeline:** 8 months
**Team Size:** 12 members (4 backend, 4 frontend, 2 mobile, 1 UI/UX, 1 QA)
**Budget:** $200,000

**Questions:**
1. Which SDLC model would you choose and why?
2. Create a high-level project plan with phases and milestones.
3. Identify at least 8 major risks and their mitigation strategies.
4. How would you structure the team and assign responsibilities?
5. What quality assurance measures would you implement?
6. How would you handle scope changes during development?

### Case Study 2: Legacy System Migration
**Scenario:** A banking organization wants to migrate their 15-year-old legacy system to a modern cloud-based architecture. The system handles:
- Customer accounts (5 million users)
- Transaction processing (1 million daily transactions)
- Loan management
- Report generation

**Constraints:**
- Zero downtime requirement
- Data integrity is critical
- Regulatory compliance must be maintained
- Existing staff needs training

**Questions:**
1. What are the major challenges in this project?
2. Develop a migration strategy with phases.
3. How would you ensure data integrity during migration?
4. What testing strategy would you employ?
5. Create a risk management plan specific to this migration.
6. How would you manage stakeholder expectations?

### Case Study 3: Startup Product Development
**Scenario:** A startup wants to develop a food delivery app to compete with existing players. They have:
- Limited budget ($50,000)
- 3-month timeline to MVP
- Small team (5 developers)
- Need to pivot quickly based on market feedback

**Questions:**
1. Which development methodology would you recommend?
2. How would you prioritize features for MVP?
3. Create a sprint plan for the first month.
4. What metrics would you track to measure success?
5. How would you handle technical debt in a fast-paced environment?
6. Describe your approach to continuous deployment.

## Section E: Practical/Scenario-Based Questions

1. **Resource Conflict:** Two critical tasks require the same senior developer simultaneously. How would you resolve this conflict?

2. **Budget Overrun:** Your project is 60% complete but has consumed 80% of the budget. What actions would you take?

3. **Team Member Leaving:** Your lead developer resigns mid-project. How do you handle knowledge transfer and project continuity?

4. **Stakeholder Disagreement:** The client and end-users have conflicting requirements. How would you resolve this?

5. **Technical Debt:** Your team wants to refactor code, but the client wants new features. How do you balance this?

6. **Missed Deadline:** You realize the project will miss its deadline by 3 weeks. How do you communicate this to stakeholders?

7. **Quality vs. Speed:** Management pressures you to release early, but QA has found critical bugs. What's your approach?

8. **Scope Creep:** The client keeps adding "small" features without formal change requests. How do you handle this?

9. **Team Conflict:** Two team members have a personality clash affecting productivity. What's your resolution strategy?

10. **Technology Change:** Midway through the project, a better technology emerges. Should you switch? How do you decide?

## Tips for Exam Preparation

1. **Understand Concepts:** Don't just memorize; understand the 'why' behind each methodology.
2. **Practice Diagrams:** Be comfortable drawing Gantt charts, PERT diagrams, and network diagrams.
3. **Real-world Examples:** Relate concepts to real software projects you've worked on.
4. **Case Studies:** Practice solving case studies within time limits.
5. **Formulas:** Memorize key formulas (COCOMO, PERT, Function Points).
6. **Current Trends:** Stay updated on modern practices like DevOps, CI/CD, and Agile at scale.
7. **Tools Knowledge:** Familiarize yourself with tools like JIRA, Trello, MS Project, Git.
